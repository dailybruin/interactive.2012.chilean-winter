Aliza Sepulvo

Honoring a Fallen Student

Aliza Sepulvo held a sign at an Aug. 24 candlelight vigil for Manuel Gutierrez, a high school student who died as a result of protest violence.

Sixteen-year-old Gutierrez died on Aug. 25, 2011 when a policeman shot him near a student demonstration during a two-day education strike.

Police said they fired because they saw protesters shooting at them.

Sepulvo said he came to the vigil because police violence toward civilians reminds him of a time he’d rather not remember: Chile’s years under dictator Augusto Pinochet.

“Police didn’t kill Manuel for doing something illegal; they killed him for being a student,” Sepulvo said. “People have to be conscious of that, because we can’t let this generation be a repeat of what happened during the dictatorship.”
