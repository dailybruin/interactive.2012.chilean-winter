Dalia Coto

Marching Through Gas

Dalia Coto held a sliced lemon, a remedy for the burning throat sensation that comes with tear gas.

Police use gas and water cannons to disperse lingering protesters after a march’s designated end time. Coto, a psychology student at the University of Chile, stood nearby when police threw a gas canister into the crowd at the Aug. 28 march for education.

Her eyes still glistening with tears, Soto said marches are a way to show Chileans that student protesters are a bigger population than the press makes them out to be.

“I know at the end [of a march] there will be fights with police, but that doesn’t scare me,” she said. “The message we’re sending the government is that we’re not the minority; we’re the majority.”
